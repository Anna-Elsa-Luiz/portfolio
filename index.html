The Immigration and Nationality Act (INA) of the US permits foreign workers to come to the United States to work on either a temporary or permanent basis. The act also protects US workers against adverse impacts on working place and maintain requirements when they hire foreign workers to fill workforce shortages. The immigration programs are administered by the Office of Foreign Labor Certification (OFLC).





OFLC gives job certification applications for employers seeking to bring foreign workers into the United States and grants certifications. As In last year the count of employees were huge so OFLC needs Machine learning models to shortlist visa applicants based on their previous data.

In this project we are going to use the data given to build a Classification model:

  • This model is to check if Visa get approved or not based on the given dataset.
  • This can be used to Recommend a suitable profile for the applicants for whom the visa should be certified or denied based on the certain criteria which influences the decision.



    • Named Entity Recognition

    Objective

    The primary objective of using the bert-base-cased model for Named Entity Recognition (NER) is to automatically identify and classify named entities within a text corpus. These entities can be people, organizations, locations, dates, monetary values, or other predefined categories depending on your specific needs.





    Problem Statement/Case Study

    Imagine you have a large collection of customer reviews for a retail company. You want to automatically identify the names of products mentioned in the reviews to understand customer sentiment and buying trends. Manually identifying all product names in the reviews would be time-consuming and error-prone.

    Solution

    Here's how you can use the bert-base-cased model for NER to solve this problem

  • 1. Data Preparation
  • Annotate a sample of your customer reviews with the named entities you want to identify (e.g., product names).
  • Preprocess your text data by cleaning and tokenizing it for the model.
  • 2. Model Training
  • Fine-tune the bert-base-cased model on your annotated dataset. This involves adjusting the model's weights to specialize it for NER on your specific domain (customer reviews).
  • 3. Entity Recognition
  • Use the fine-tuned model to process your entire collection of customer reviews.
  • The model will identify and classify product names (and potentially other entities) within the reviews.
  • 4. Analysis and Insights
  • o Analyze the extracted product names to understand customer preferences, identify popular items, and gain insights into product sentiment.
